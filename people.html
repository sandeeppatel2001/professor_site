NANO-MATERIALS LAB Members



Mr. Priyam Srivastava

Associate Professor
Mr. Priyam Srivastava is pursuing a Ph.D. in Materials Engineering at IIT-Jammu and joined the Nano-materials lab in fall 2022. He also serves as the coordinator of the Indian Sakura Science Club association (ISSCA). His primary research area is related to thermal management materials for electronics applications. He is working to develop high thermal conductive materials with excellent mechanical properties. During M.Tech. (2020-22) in Materials Science and Technology, CUTN, he has developed an MXene-based flexible bio-sensor that can be used for strain measurement, including in-situ applications. As an undergraduate B.Tech. (2016-20) in Mechanical Engineering, he designed a solar racing vehicle and led a team of 40 members to participate in the ESVC-2020 solar vehicle event. He has work experience (two months) in Yoshida Inc. Ltd. Japan as a Mechanical Engineer funded by METI Government of Japan (2020). He has participated in a research exchange program (2018) at Kyushu Institute of Technology, Japan (for ten days), sponsored by Japan Science and Technology. He has gained experience in synthesis, characterization of materials, CAD modeling, and other computational analysis.
Ph.D.: Mechanical Engineering(Major: Materials, Minor: Design), University of Southern California, Los Angeles, 2007
M.S.: Mechanical Engineering (Mechanics and Materials), University of Southern California, Los Angeles , 2005
B. Tech.: Mechanical Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur, 2003

Sravan S Sankar

Mr Sravan S Sankar is a fourth-year B.Tech student in the materials engineering department at IIT Jammu. His current BTech project is "Finite Element Mesh Analysis for Force mapping in the context of Ni-based Magneto-Optic System: ANSYS Formulation and Simulation," in which he investigated the micro-mechanics of force distribution to the ribbon samples through a finite element-based mesh approach under various simulated loading conditions along with the high-resolution Transmission Electron Microscopy assisted microstructural analysis. He has an experience as a research intern during his summer internship at the Centre for Materials for Electronics Technology, Pune, where he worked on "Synthesis of Manganese and Vanadium oxide as an Electrode Material for Electrochemical Energy Storage."
